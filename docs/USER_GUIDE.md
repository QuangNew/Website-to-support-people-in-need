## 1. Getting Started

### 1.1 Creating an Account

1. Navigate to the ReliefConnect registration page
2. Fill in:
   - **Username** — Your unique identifier on the platform
   - **Email** — A valid email address for verification
   - **Full Name** — Your display name
   - **Password** — Minimum 8 characters with at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, and one digit
3. Click **Register**
4. Check your email for a **6-digit verification code** (arrives within 1–2 minutes)
5. Enter the code on the verification screen within **15 minutes**

> **Tip**: You can also register using **Google Sign-In** by clicking the Google button on the login page. This automatically verifies your email.

### 1.2 Logging In

- Enter your **email or username** and password
- Alternatively, click **Sign in with Google**
- After login, you'll be directed to the interactive relief map

### 1.3 Resetting Your Password

1. Click **Forgot Password** on the login page
2. Enter your registered email
3. Check your email for a 6-digit reset code
4. Enter the code and set a new password

## 3. Relief Map

The interactive map is the core of ReliefConnect, displaying live humanitarian data across Vietnam.

### 3.1 Map Markers

| Marker Type | Color | Description |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| **SOS Request** | Red | Active emergency or help request |
| **Supply Warehouse** | Blue | Available relief supplies |
| **Safe Shelter** | Green | Safe locations and shelters |

**Blinking markers** indicate SOS requests that have been unconfirmed for more than 15 minutes — these are highest priority.

### 3.2 Viewing Details

Click any map marker to open the **Ping Detail Panel** showing:
- Type and current status (Pending, In Progress, Resolved, Verified Safe)
- Description of the emergency or resource
- Creator information
- Assigned volunteer (if any)
- Timestamps and location coordinates

### 3.3 Creating an SOS Request

> Requires: Authenticated account

1. Click the **Create SOS** button on the map interface
2. Click on the map to set your location, or enter coordinates manually
3. Select the request type: **SOS**, **Supply**, or **Shelter**
4. Provide a detailed description of your situation
5. Click **Submit**

Your SOS request will immediately appear on all users' maps. If not confirmed within 15 minutes, it will start blinking to attract volunteer attention.

### 3.4 Confirming Safety

If you created an SOS request and are now safe:
1. Open your SOS ping on the map
2. Click **Confirm Safe**
3. The blinking alert will stop and volunteers will be notified

### 3.5 Routing & Navigation

Click on any marker and select **Get Directions** to see turn-by-turn navigation:
- Two route alternatives are shown: fastest and shortest
- Click on either route line to select it
- Route distances and estimated times are displayed

### 3.6 Filtering

Use the **Filter Bar** at the top of the map to:
- Show/hide specific marker types (SOS, Supply, Shelter)
- Filter by status (Pending, In Progress, Resolved)
- Search by area

## 4. Social Feed

The community social network lets users share their stories and connect with people who can help.

### 4.1 Browsing Posts

- Posts are displayed in a scrolling feed, newest first
- Each post shows: author name, role badge, category label, time, reaction counts, and comment count
- Scroll down to load more posts automatically (lazy loading)

### 4.2 Filtering Posts

Use the filter options to narrow the feed:
- **By Category**: Livelihood, Medical, Education
- **By Author Role**: Person in Need, Volunteer, Sponsor
- **By Sort Order**: Newest, Most Reactions

### 4.3 Creating a Post

> Requires: Authenticated account

1. Click the **compose** button at the top of the social feed
2. Write your story or request
3. Select a **category**:
   - **Livelihood** — Economic hardship, family crises, loss of home/employment
   - **Medical** — Health emergencies, chronic conditions, medical costs
   - **Education** — School supply needs, tuition assistance, educational barriers
4. Optionally attach an **image** (JPEG, PNG, or WebP; max 5 MB)
5. Click **Post**

### 4.4 Reacting to Posts

Click one of the three reaction buttons on any post:
- **👍 Like** — General support
- **❤️ Love** — Deep empathy
- **🙏 Pray** — Thoughts and prayers

Click the same reaction again to remove it. Click a different reaction to switch.

### 4.5 Commenting

1. Click the **comment** section on any post to expand it
2. Type your comment in the input field
3. Press **Send**
4. Comments are displayed chronologically below the post

### 4.6 Viewing a User's Wall

Click on any user's name to visit their personal wall — a timeline of all their posts.

### 4.7 Reporting Content

If you see a post that violates platform policies:
1. Click the **Report** button on the post
2. Provide a reason for the report
3. The report enters the admin moderation queue for review

## 5. AI Chatbot

The AI assistant helps you with emergency guidance, platform usage, and disaster information.

### 5.1 Starting a Conversation

1. Navigate to the **Chat** section via the sidebar
2. The chatbot greets you automatically in your selected language
3. Type your question or message in the input field
4. Press **Send** or hit Enter

### 5.2 What the Chatbot Can Help With

- **Emergency first aid**: "How to stop bleeding", "What to do for a heart attack"
- **Survival guidance**: "How to prepare for a typhoon", "Flood safety tips"
- **Platform help**: "How do I create an SOS request?", "How to verify my account?"
- **Disaster information**: "Current typhoon warnings", "Nearest shelter location"
- **General questions**: The chatbot can answer a wide range of topics within the humanitarian domain

### 5.3 Sending Images

You can attach an image to your message for the chatbot to analyze:
- Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP
- Maximum size: 4 MB
- Use cases: "What is the damage level in this photo?", "Can you identify this medical condition?"

### 5.4 Safety Warnings

When the chatbot detects emergency keywords (e.g., "heart attack", "bleeding", "cấp cứu"), it automatically:
- Adds a **safety warning** indicator to the response
- Provides Vietnamese emergency numbers: **113** (Police), **114** (Fire), **115** (Medical)
- Prioritizes actionable first-aid instructions

### 5.5 Conversation Management

- **Message History**: Your conversations are saved on the server and cached locally (up to 200 messages)
- **New Chat**: Click the **Reset** button to start a fresh conversation (you'll be asked to confirm)
- **Multiple Conversations**: Each "New Chat" creates a separate conversation on the server

## 6. Volunteer Guide

> Requires: Volunteer role (verified by admin)

### 6.1 Becoming a Volunteer

1. Create an account and verify your email
2. Go to **Profile → Verify**
3. Select **Volunteer** as your target role
4. Provide your phone number, address, and supporting documents
5. Wait for administrator approval (you'll see the status update in your profile)

### 6.2 Browsing Available Tasks

1. Navigate to the **Map** section
2. Use the volunteer task panel to see pending SOS requests
3. Tasks are sorted by proximity to your location

### 6.3 Accepting a Task

1. Click on an SOS ping marker on the map
2. Review the emergency details
3. Click **Accept Task**
4. The ping status changes to **In Progress** and is assigned to you

### 6.4 Completing a Task

1. Navigate to the location and provide assistance
2. Open your active task from the panel
3. Update the status to **Resolved** and add completion notes
4. The person in need will be notified

### 6.5 Real-Time Alerts

As a volunteer, you automatically receive **real-time SOS alerts** via WebSocket:
- Blinking markers on the map indicate unconfirmed emergencies (> 15 minutes)
- Audio/visual notifications when new high-priority SOS requests appear
- Alerts clear automatically when resolved

## 8. Profile & Verification

### 8.1 Viewing Your Profile

Navigate to **Profile** via the sidebar to see:
- Your account information (name, email, username)
- Your current role and verification status
- Your phone number and address (if provided)

### 8.2 Editing Your Profile

Click **Edit Profile** to update:
- Full name
- Phone number
- Address
- Avatar image

### 8.3 Changing Your Password

1. Go to **Profile → Change Password**
2. Enter your current password
3. Enter and confirm your new password
4. Click **Save**

### 8.4 Role Verification

To request an upgraded role:

1. Go to **Profile → Verify** (or click **Verify** in the sidebar)
2. Select your desired role:
   - **Person in Need** — Access to create SOS requests and post stories
   - **Volunteer** — Access to accept and manage relief tasks
   - **Sponsor** — Access to view cases and offer help
3. Fill in:
   - **Phone Number** — Your contact number
   - **Address** — Your residential address
   - **Documents** — Supporting evidence (ID card, proof of need, organizational credentials)
4. Submit and wait for admin review

**Verification Statuses**:
- `None` — No verification submitted
- `Pending` — Under admin review
- `Approved` — Role upgraded
- `Rejected` — Submission rejected (reason provided; you may re-submit)

## 9. Administrator Guide

> Requires: Admin role (internally assigned)

### 9.1 Accessing the Admin Panel

Click **Admin** in the sidebar to access the administration dashboard with 10 tabs.

### 9.2 Dashboard Statistics

The **Stats** tab provides a real-time overview:
- Total users broken down by role
- SOS request counts by status
- Post and comment counts
- Active announcements

### 9.3 Verification Queue

The **Verifications** tab shows pending role requests:
1. Review user's submitted documents, phone, and address
2. Click **Approve** to upgrade the user's role
3. Or click **Reject** and provide a reason

### 9.4 User Management

The **Users** tab allows full user lifecycle management:
- **Search & Filter**: Find users by name, email, role, or verification status
- **Suspend**: Temporarily disable an account (set an expiry date)
- **Unsuspend**: Restore a suspended account
- **Ban**: Permanently disable an account (record a reason)
- **Force Logout**: Invalidate all active sessions immediately
- **Reset Verification**: Allow a user to re-submit verification

### 9.5 Content Moderation

The **Posts** tab:
- Browse all platform posts with pagination
- **Pin** important posts to the top of the social feed
- **Delete** posts that violate policies

The **Reports** tab:
- Review community-submitted reports
- Mark as **Reviewed** (take action) or **Dismiss** (no action needed)

### 9.6 Audit Logs

The **Logs** tab provides a complete audit trail:
- All administrative actions are logged with timestamps
- Batch operations show as expandable parent entries with child logs
- Export logs to CSV for external review

### 9.7 Announcements

The **Announcements** tab:
- Create platform-wide announcements with title, content, and optional expiry date
- Edit or delete existing announcements
- Active announcements display to all users

### 9.8 Zone Management

The **Zones** tab:
- Create priority zones by drawing GeoJSON polygons on the map
- Set risk levels (1–5)
- Zones appear as color-coded overlays on the relief map

### 9.9 Supply Management

The **Supply** tab:
- Add supply warehouse locations with items and quantities
- Edit or remove supply entries
- Supply markers appear on the relief map

### 9.10 API Key Management

The **API Keys** tab:
- Add new Gemini API keys to the rotation pool
- Monitor usage counts per key
- Activate or deactivate keys
- The system automatically load-balances across active keys

### 9.11 Batch Operations

For bulk actions:
1. Select multiple items (verifications, posts, users)
2. Choose the batch action (approve, reject, delete)
3. Confirm the batch operation
4. A parent log entry is created with individual child logs for each item

## 10. Troubleshooting & FAQ

### Q: I didn't receive the verification email
**A**: Check your spam/junk folder. The code expires after 15 minutes — if expired, try registering again or request a new code. Emails are sent via Gmail SMTP and may occasionally be delayed.

### Q: My SOS request is blinking — what does that mean?
**A**: A blinking SOS marker means the request has been unconfirmed for over 15 minutes. If you're safe, open the request and click **Confirm Safe** to stop the alert.

### Q: The map doesn't show any markers
**A**: Ensure your network connection is stable. Check that filters aren't hiding marker types. If the issue persists, try refreshing the page.

### Q: My role verification was rejected — can I try again?
**A**: Yes. After rejection, you can view the reason in your profile and re-submit with corrected or additional documentation.

### Q: The chatbot isn't responding
**A**: The AI chatbot has a rate limit of 30 messages per 5 minutes. If you've exceeded this, wait a few minutes. If the issue persists, the AI backend may be temporarily unavailable.

### Q: Can I delete my account?
**A**: Account deletion is planned for a future update. Contact an administrator for manual account removal.

### Q: How do I report a bug or suggest a feature?
**A**: Use the AI chatbot to describe the issue, or contact the platform administrators through the social feed.

### Q: What browsers are supported?
**A**: ReliefConnect works best on modern browsers — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari (latest versions). Internet Explorer is not supported.

### Q: Is my location data shared?
**A**: Your SOS ping coordinates are visible on the map to all users. Map routing requests may use the OSRM public server. See the [Privacy Policy](POLICIES.md#2-privacy--data-protection-policy) for full details.
